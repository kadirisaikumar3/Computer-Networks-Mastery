# Introduction

## What is a Firewall?

A **Firewall** is a network security device or software that monitors, filters, and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predefined security rules.

It acts as a barrier between a trusted internal network and an untrusted external network such as the Internet.

---

# Simple Definition

A Firewall protects a computer or network by allowing legitimate traffic and blocking unauthorized or malicious traffic.

---

# Why is a Firewall Important?

Without a Firewall:

- Unauthorized users may access the network.
- Malware can enter the system.
- Sensitive information may be exposed.
- Cyber attacks become easier.

Firewalls provide the first line of defense for a network.

---
