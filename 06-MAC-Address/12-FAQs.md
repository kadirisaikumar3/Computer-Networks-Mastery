# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## 1. What is a MAC Address?

A MAC Address is a unique physical address assigned to a Network Interface Card (NIC).

---

## 2. What is the size of a MAC Address?

48 Bits (6 Bytes).

---

## 3. Which OSI layer uses the MAC Address?

Layer 2 (Data Link Layer).

---

## 4. Who assigns a MAC Address?

The manufacturer using an OUI allocated by IEEE.

---

## 5. Can a MAC Address change?

Yes.

It can be changed temporarily using software (MAC Spoofing).

---

## 6. What is the Broadcast MAC Address?

```
FF:FF:FF:FF:FF:FF
```

---

## 7. What is OUI?

The first 24 bits of a MAC Address that identify the manufacturer.

---

## 8. What is ARP?

ARP maps an IP Address to its corresponding MAC Address.

---

## 9. Does a switch use MAC Addresses?

Yes.

A switch forwards Ethernet frames using MAC Addresses.

---

## 10. Why is a MAC Address important?

It uniquely identifies devices and enables communication within a Local Area Network.
