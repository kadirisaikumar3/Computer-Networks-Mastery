# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## 1. What is TCP?

Transmission Control Protocol.

---

## 2. Which layer does TCP work on?

Transport Layer.

---

## 3. Is TCP reliable?

Yes.

---

## 4. Is TCP connection-oriented?

Yes.

---

## 5. Why is TCP called reliable?

Because it uses acknowledgments, retransmissions, sequence numbers, checksums, and timers.

---

## 6. What is the Three-Way Handshake?

A process used to establish a TCP connection.

Steps:

- SYN
- SYN + ACK
- ACK

---

## 7. What is Four-Way Termination?

A process used to close a TCP connection.

Steps:

- FIN
- ACK
- FIN
- ACK

---

## 8. What is Flow Control?

A mechanism that prevents the sender from overwhelming the receiver.

---

## 9. What is Congestion Control?

A mechanism that prevents network congestion.

---

## 10. What is the Sliding Window?

A TCP mechanism used for Flow Control.

---

## 11. What is a Sequence Number?

It identifies the position of transmitted data.

---

## 12. What is an ACK Number?

It specifies the next expected byte.

---

## 13. What is a Checksum?

It detects transmission errors.

---

## 14. Which applications use TCP?

- HTTP
- HTTPS
- FTP
- SMTP
- POP3
- IMAP
- SSH

---

## 15. What is the TCP Header size?

Minimum:

20 Bytes

Maximum:

60 Bytes
